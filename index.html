Aleksei Navalny

„This madness will not end if we remain silent!“

Here's some main events in Alexei's life so far


  • Born on June 4, 1976 in the town of Butyn in the Moscow Region.

  • In 1998 he graduated from the Faculty of Law of the Peoples' Friendship University of Russia. In 1999, he studied in absentia at the Faculty of Finance and Credit of the Financial Academy under the Government of the Russian Federation. In 2010, he completed a six-month course at Yale University.

  • Alexei began political activity in 2000, joining and becoming an active member of the Yabloko party. In 2003, Navalny was in charge of Yabloko's Duma election campaign in Moscow. Since 2006, he has been a member of the party's federal council.

  • In 2008, he began his anti-corruption activities by publishing an article on the social network LiveJournal about money fraud in the Russian company Gazprom. On the wave of support for his activities, the RosPil project was created, which is engaged in identifying abuses in Russian public procurement.

  • In 2013, Navalny announced his candidacy for the mayoral elections in Moscow, eventually gaining 27% of the vote, which exceeded the expectations of many officials.

  • Since 2015, he has been actively producing investigative films about corruption among Russian authorities. The most famous among such films were videos about the former President of Russia Dmitry Medvedev and about the palace of the current President Vladimir Putin .

  • On August 20, 2020, Navalny, returning by plane from Tomsk to Moscow, lost consciousness and fell into a coma. He was urgently hospitalized and a few days later he was taken to Germany for treatment. Several independent laboratories at once confirmed later that the politician was poisoned with a combat poison.

  • On January 17, 2021, Alexey returned to Russia and was immediately arrested at the airport upon arrival in Moscow. Since then, until today, Alexei has been in prison.
